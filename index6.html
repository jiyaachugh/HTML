It's me, Mario

Mario Club is a debugging subsidiary under Nintendo's Licensing Division in Kyoto. It is sometimes referred to as the Kyoto research center. The club plays a crucial role in ensuring the quality and functionality of Nintendo games.

Join Today!

In-game credits, the Super Mario Club is often credited under different names, including Super Mario Club, (Super) Mario Club staff, NCL Super Mario Club, and Super Mario Club Debug Team. They also appear in the special thanks section.Mario Club has an interesting history. Originally a debugging team within Nintendo, it officially became a subsidiary in July 2009. As of September 2021, Mario Club employs 343 people. Nintendo fully owns Mario Club, and its president is Masanori Okazaki. Additionally, “Super Mario Club” was the name of a long-running Nintendo-sponsored Japanese variety show that aired from 1990 to 2001. The show had various incarnations, including “Super Mario Stadium”, “64 Mario Stadium”, and “Mario School”.
